Plan 9

The goal of Plan 9 was to build a system that was centrally administered and cost-effective using cheap modern microcomputers as its computing elements. The idea was to build a time-sharing system using different computers that handle different tasks. Small machines in your office would serve as terminals to large, central, shared resources such as computing servers and file servers. Plan 9 is highly influenced by the Cambridge Distributed System. The early catch phrase was to build a UNIX out of a lot of little systems, not a system out of a lot of little UNIXes....
